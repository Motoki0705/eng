## 選択肢の正誤解説

| 番号 | 正誤 | 選択肢 | 解説 |
|:---:|:---:|--------|------|
| 11 | ✗ | Jobs's adoptive parents were wealthy professionals who could easily afford college tuition. | 本文では"a working-class couple"と明記されており、裕福な専門職ではない。 |
| 12 | ✗ | Jobs completed his college education at Reed College as his biological mother had wished. | 本文では"he dropped out after just one semester"とあり、1学期で中退した。 |
| 13 | ○ | A calligraphy class Jobs took in college later influenced the design of Apple products. | 本文に"a calligraphy course that would later influence the typography of Apple computers"と明記。 |
| 14 | ✗ | Jobs founded Apple Computer by himself before later bringing in Steve Wozniak. | 本文では"he co-founded Apple Computer with his friend Steve Wozniak"とあり、最初から共同創業。 |
| 15 | ✗ | Jobs voluntarily left Apple in order to start new business ventures. | 本文では"internal power struggles at Apple led to Jobs being forced out"とあり、追い出された。自主的に離れたのではない。 |
| 16 | ○ | Toy Story was the first feature film to be made entirely using computer animation. | 本文に"the first entirely computer-animated feature film"と明記。 |
| 17 | ✗ | Jobs sold Pixar before it had produced any successful films. | 本文ではToy Storyの成功の後、"Jobs eventually sold Pixar to Disney"とあり、成功後に売却した。 |
| 18 | ○ | Apple acquired NeXT in order to bring Jobs back to the company. | 本文に"the company acquired NeXT, bringing Jobs back"と明記。 |
| 19 | ✗ | Jobs was known for being easy-going and relaxed with his employees. | 本文では"demanding perfectionism"と"ability to inspire both devotion and fear"とあり、厳しかった。 |
| 20 | ○ | Jobs continued working at Apple until just before his death from cancer. | 本文に"He continued working until shortly before his death"と明記。 |

## 読解のポイント

### 1. 時系列の把握
- 1955年：サンフランシスコで誕生
- 1976年：Apple Computer共同創業
- 1977年：Apple II発売
- 1984年：Macintosh発売
- Apple追放後：NeXTとPixarを設立
- 1995年：Toy Story公開
- 1997年：Appleに復帰
- 2003年：膵臓がん診断
- 2011年：死去（56歳）

### 2. 因果関係の正確な把握
- "forced out" ≠ "voluntarily left"
- Pixar売却のタイミング = Toy Story成功後
