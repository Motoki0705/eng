The concept of "social capital" has become increasingly important in modern sociology. Researchers have discovered that communities with strong social networks tend to be healthier and more prosperous. **Those who actively participate in community activities not only benefit themselves but also contribute to what we call the "collective well-being" of their neighborhoods.** This finding has led many local governments to invest in programs that encourage citizen participation.
