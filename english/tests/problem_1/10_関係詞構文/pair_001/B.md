Language acquisition in children has fascinated scientists for decades. Unlike adults, young children seem to absorb new languages effortlessly. **What makes this process so remarkable is that children can master complex grammatical rules without any formal instruction, which suggests that humans are born with an innate capacity for language.** This theory, proposed by linguist Noam Chomsky, remains influential in the field of cognitive science.
