The history of photography reveals fascinating changes in how people perceive reality. In the early days, photographs were considered objective records of truth. **The camera, the lens of which could capture scenes with unprecedented accuracy, was believed to be an instrument that never lied, unlike painters whose interpretations of reality were inevitably subjective.** However, modern scholars have challenged this assumption, arguing that the photographer's choices of subject, angle, and timing shape the meaning of every image.
